The most useful productivity technique I’ve found is also the simplest: pick
one activity that would make the day feel meaningful if it were the only thing
that got done.

## Why one thing helps

Endless task lists create the feeling of progress without requiring a decision
about what matters. A daily highlight makes the decision explicit. It can be a
milestone on a long project, an urgent appointment, or something satisfying
that you have been putting off.

The constraint is useful because it turns a vague day into a small promise. I
usually give the highlight 60 to 90 minutes, put it on the calendar, and let
the rest of the day remain flexible.

## A small practice

Ask: “What would make me feel good about today when I look back tonight?” Write
the answer somewhere visible, then start before opening the rest of the task
list.
